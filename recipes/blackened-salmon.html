Blackened Salmon

Description

This blackened Salmon recipe is bold and delicious in just 15 minutes! This versatile dish can be paired with any number of sides. It's impressive enough to serve guests, but easy enough for a weeknight meal.

My favorite thing about this recipe is that it only requires a few simple ingredients. Things don't have to be complicated to be delicious! Enjoy the simplicity with this easy meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp. canola or light olive oil
  • 1 tsp. fine sea salt or kosher salt
  • 2-3 tbsp Homemade Blackened Seasoning (or store bought)
  • 4 6-ounce salmon fillets, skin on, bones removed
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme (leaves only)
  • 1 tsp. fresh oregano leaves
  • 1 lemon (zest & juice)

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil (to smoking) in a cast iron or similar skillet set over medium heat
  2. Sprinkle flesh side of the fillets with salt. Spread the Blackened Seasoning on a plate and press the fles-side of the salmon into the seasoning until heavily coated.
  3. When the skillet and oil are reading, remove the skillet from heat. Press the salmon fillets one at a time (flesh side down) into the oil in the skillet. Set the skillet back over medium heat and cook about 2-3 minutes or until the salmon is blackenedbut not burned. Turn the salmon fillets over and continue cooking (skin side down) until the skin is crispy, 4-6 mintutes./li>
  4. Transfer the fillets to a serving platter, sprinkle with lemon zest and juice and serve immediately. Enjoy!
